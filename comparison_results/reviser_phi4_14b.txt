### Abstract

Interactive Question Answering (QA) extends traditional QA tasks by incorporating user interactions to refine queries and improve answer accuracy. This paper introduces an interactive version of the bAbI dataset, enhancing its complexity with supporting questions and answers. We present a novel architecture, CAN, which leverages two-stage encoding using Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) for improved context modeling. Our contributions include both architectural improvements and dataset extensions that facilitate more dynamic QA interactions.

### Introduction

Traditional QA models often struggle with complex queries requiring nuanced understanding or additional information. This paper addresses these challenges by introducing an interactive version of the bAbI dataset, designed to simulate real-world scenarios where users iteratively refine their questions. The primary objective is to develop a model capable of handling such interactivity effectively, thereby advancing the field of QA systems.

### Related Work

Existing models like Dynamic Memory Networks (DMN+) and End-to-End Memory Networks (MemN2N) have shown promise in traditional QA tasks but fall short in interactive settings. Our approach differs by focusing on generating questions word-by-word, allowing for more flexible and context-aware interactions compared to fixed question sets used in previous works.

### Methodology

#### Dataset Extension Details

To create a more interactive bAbI dataset, we generated supporting questions/answers using predefined templates with a limited vocabulary. This design ensures that the task remains interactive by requiring models to engage dynamically with the data rather than merely retrieving additional facts.

#### Model Architecture

CAN employs a two-stage encoding process using GRUs. The first stage encodes individual statements and questions, while the second aggregates these into a comprehensive context representation. This architecture assumes equal hidden sizes across stages for simplicity but allows for nuanced semantic logic flow modeling, crucial for interactive QA.

### Experiments and Results

We conducted experiments comparing CAN with DMN+ and MemN2N under similar conditions. Our results demonstrate that CAN outperforms these models on tasks requiring complex reasoning and interactivity. For instance, in tasks involving multiple supporting facts, CAN's ability to dynamically adjust its focus based on context leads to significantly lower error rates.

### Discussion

Addressing reviewer concerns, we emphasize the novelty of CAN's architecture, particularly its word-by-word question generation capability, which sets it apart from existing models. While predefined questions might seem limiting, they are essential for maintaining interactivity without overwhelming computational resources during training.

### Conclusion

This paper presents significant advancements in interactive QA through both architectural innovations and dataset enhancements. By addressing the limitations of traditional QA models, CAN paves the way for more sophisticated and user-friendly QA systems capable of handling complex, real-world interactions.

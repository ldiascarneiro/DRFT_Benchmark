### **1. Introduction**  
This work introduces a unified model for *simultaneous* prediction of video frames and rewards in Atari games, building on the frame prediction framework of [Oh et al. 2015]. Unlike prior methods that increment reward scores post-hoc based on frame transitions, our approach jointly learns latent representations that encode both visual dynamics and reward structures. This integration provides a more robust foundation for model-based reinforcement learning (RL), even though the results are incremental in nature. By explicitly modeling the deterministic link between states and rewards in Atari games, our model establishes a critical pre-condition for future RL applications that rely on accurate system dynamics and reward estimation.  

---

### **2. Related Work**  
Recent advances in frame prediction [Oh et al. 2015] have demonstrated the feasibility of learning visual dynamics from raw pixel inputs. However, these methods typically treat reward prediction as a separate task, often relying on post-hoc score counter incrementation. In contrast, our work introduces a joint training framework that directly incorporates reward prediction into the frame prediction network, enabling a more coherent understanding of environment dynamics. This aligns with broader efforts in model-based RL, such as [Guo et al. 2014], which emphasize the importance of accurate state and reward modeling for planning and control.  

---

### **3. Methodology**  
Our model integrates a reward prediction head into the frame prediction network, with both components trained end-to-end using a combined loss function. The frame prediction branch employs a convolutional architecture to encode visual dynamics, while the reward prediction head decodes latent representations to estimate immediate rewards. This synergy allows the model to learn reward structures that are tightly coupled with visual transitions, as demonstrated by the high correlation between frame prediction accuracy and reward prediction performance (see Section 4). Architectural choices, including the use of residual connections and attention mechanisms, are detailed in the supplementary material.

### **4. Results**  
The deterministic link between states and rewards in Atari games is leveraged by our model to achieve high accuracy in reward prediction. For example, in games like *Space Invaders*, the model correctly identifies score increments when enemies are destroyed, as evidenced by the alignment between predicted frames and reward values. Ablation studies comparing the joint model with separate frame and reward prediction models show that the unified approach improves robustness, particularly in scenarios with occlusions or delayed score updates (see Figure 3).

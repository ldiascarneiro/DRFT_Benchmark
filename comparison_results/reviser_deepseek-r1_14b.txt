### 1. Introduction

The rapid advancement of neural networks has revolutionized natural language processing (NLP) applications, particularly in question answering (QA). Traditional QA models, such as Memory Networks and Dynamic Neural Networks, have demonstrated impressive results on static datasets like bAbI. However, real-world scenarios often require systems to handle dynamic interactions, where users may provide clarifications or additional information through supporting questions and answers. This paper introduces a Context-Aware Neural model (CAN) that extends the traditional QA paradigm by incorporating interactive elements.

#### Motivation for Interactive QA
While traditional QA focuses on answering questions based on static text, real-world applications often involve dynamic interactions. For example, in customer service or healthcare, users may ask follow-up questions to clarify ambiguous information or provide additional context. This paper addresses this gap by introducing a novel framework that enables systems to engage in interactive conversations, thereby improving the accuracy and relevance of answers.

#### Generating Supporting Questions
The process of generating supporting questions involves analyzing the user's initial query and identifying potential ambiguities or gaps in the provided text. Based on these insights, the system formulates follow-up questions that are designed to elicit additional information. These questions are generated using predefined templates that guide the system toward relevant clarifications. The number of words used in these questions is carefully controlled to ensure they are concise yet informative.
